I study how interaction patterns and participation structures shape learning processes in collaborative and data-rich learning environments.

My research examines how learners participate, take on roles, and build knowledge through interaction. Using social network analysis and interpretable computational methods, I investigate how these patterns become visible in interaction data and how they relate to engagement, knowledge construction, and student success. Across this work, I use analytic evidence not only to understand learning processes, but also to inform the design and improvement of learning environments.

Selected Publications by Research Trajectory

Knowledge Structures and Collaborative Meaning-Making

Early work in this area examined how learners organize ideas and develop shared understanding through external representations and collaborative concept mapping.

  • Lee, M., & Clariana, R. B. (2022). The influence of external concept structures on an individual’s knowledge structures. Educational Technology Research and Development, 70(5), 1657–1674.
  • Lee, M., Garbrick, A., & Clariana, R. (2021). Knowledge convergence in collaborative concept mapping. Association for Educational Communications & Technology (AECT).
  • Lee, M., Garbrick, A., & Clariana, R. (2021). Lexical network analysis on synchronous discussion. Association for Educational Communications & Technology (AECT).

Interaction Patterns and Participation Structures

This line of work investigates how group formation, network structure, and communication patterns shape participation and engagement in collaborative and online learning environments.

  • Lee, M., & Sharma, P. (2024). Applying a community detection algorithm to examine group formation in online discussions: Exploring network characteristics and dynamics. International Journal of Educational Technology in Higher Education.
  • Lee, M., & Sharma, P. (2025). Network structure and communication clarity as factors influencing engagement in asynchronous online discussions. Interactive Learning Environments.
  • Lee, M., & Clariana, R. (In Revision). Implementing sociograms in online learning: examining their influence on community of inquiry elements. Learning Environments Research.
  • Sharma, P., & Lee, M. (In Revision). The quality of student cognitive engagement across different levels of social engagement in online discussions. Education and Information Technologies.

More recent work extends interaction-focused research through role prediction, interpretable modeling, and learning analytics related to engagement and student success.

  • Lee, M., Sharma, P., & Clariana, R. (Under Review). Which roles bridge, centralize, or synthesize collaboration in online courses? Linking network metrics with knowledge construction in asynchronous discussions. International Journal of Computer-Supported Collaborative Learning.
  • Lee, M., Sharma, P., & Clariana, R. (Under Review). When does structure matter? Social and cognitive outcomes in role-assigned vs. organic online groups. Learning and Instruction.
  • Lee, M., & Tiwari, A. (Under Review). Beyond the black box: interpretable early-warning signals from first-year academic and support transitions. Journal of Learning Analytics.
