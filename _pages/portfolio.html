I study how interaction patterns and participation structures shape learning processes across collaborative and data-rich learning environments.

My research examines how learners participate, take on roles, and build knowledge through interaction. Using social network analysis and interpretable computational methods, I investigate how these patterns become visible in interaction data and how they relate to engagement, knowledge construction, and student success. Across this work, I use analytic evidence not only to understand learning processes, but also to inform the design and improvement of learning environments.

Selected Projects

Structured Roles in Collaborative Learning

Examines how assigned roles shape participation, interaction, and knowledge construction in collaborative learning environments.

Social Network Analysis of Learning Interaction

Uses network-based approaches to analyze participation structures, interaction patterns, and engagement in online learning contexts.

Knowledge Structures and Collaborative Meaning-Making

Investigates how learners organize ideas, construct shared understanding, and develop knowledge through collaborative activity.

Learning Analytics for Engagement and Student Success

Extends interaction-focused analytics to broader questions of engagement, persistence, and student success across educational settings.
