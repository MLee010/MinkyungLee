My research centers on how meaningful structures in learning shape understanding, participation, and knowledge construction, and how those structures can be made visible analytically. Across my work, I examine this question at multiple levels: through studies of how external representations influence learners’ knowledge structures, how roles and interaction patterns shape collaborative learning, and how computational approaches can identify learning-relevant patterns in educational data. More recently, this line of inquiry has expanded to interpretable machine learning and larger-scale educational data analysis, prompting a broader question that has become increasingly important in the AI era: how to use computational scale and automation in ways that remain connected to learning context, interpretive depth, and educational meaning.
