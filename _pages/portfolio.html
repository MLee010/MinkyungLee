I study how participation structures and interaction patterns shape learning processes, using network analysis and computational approaches to examine engagement, knowledge construction, and student success across learning environments.

My work brings together collaborative learning research and learning analytics to understand how interaction data can reveal patterns of participation, learner roles, and engagement. Across a series of studies, I examine how these patterns emerge in learning environments and how they can inform the design, evaluation, and improvement of educational systems.

Learning Processes in Collaborative Environments

I examine how collaborative learning unfolds through participation structures, discourse patterns, and learner roles. This line of research focuses on how learners contribute, respond to one another, and build knowledge through structured interaction in group learning environments.

Related work includes studies of structured roles in collaborative learning, knowledge construction in online discussions, and participation dynamics in collaborative settings.

Interaction Data and Learning Analytics

A second line of my work investigates how interaction data can be used to understand patterns of participation and engagement in learning environments. Using approaches such as social network analysis and interpretable computational modeling, I study how interaction structures emerge and how they relate to learning processes and student success.

This includes research on network position, learner roles, interaction modeling, and analytic approaches that connect collaborative processes with broader indicators of engagement and persistence.

Design, Evaluation, and Learning Improvement

A third strand of my work applies insights from learning analytics to the design and evaluation of learning environments. This includes using analytic evidence to inform instructional design decisions, improve collaborative learning activities, and support systems that foster student engagement and success.

Rather than treating analytics as separate from design, I approach them as a way to better understand learning environments and guide their improvement.
