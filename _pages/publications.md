## Designing Collaborative Sensemaking with Concept Mapping

**Context**  
Online and seminar-based courses focused on complex systems and design concepts.

**Instructional Design Solution**  
A two-step activity moving learners from individual concept mapping to group-based negotiation and synthesis.

**Impact**  
Supported deeper explanation of conceptual relationships and promoted shared understanding.

## Structuring High-Quality Online Discussions with Assigned Roles

**Context**  
Fully online, asynchronous undergraduate course with team-based discussion activities.

**Instructional Design Solution**  
Explicitly assigned roles (e.g., Moderator, Skeptic, Summarizer) to guide peer interaction and synthesis.

**Impact**  
Led to more distributed participation and clearer patterns of critique and integration.

## Data-Informed Group Formation to Improve Seminar Collaboration

**Context**  
Asynchronous seminar discussions with uneven participation patterns.

**Instructional Design Solution**  
Used network-informed grouping strategies to form structurally balanced discussion groups.

**Impact**  
Improved reciprocity, reduced reliance on dominant voices, and supported sustained collaboration.
